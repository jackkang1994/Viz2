GDP Analysis of Selected Countries

The gross domestic product (GDP) growth rate measures how fast the economy is growing. The rate compares the most recent quarter of the country's economic output to the previous quarter. Economic output is measured by GDP (Kimberly, 2020).

Average GDP growth in every countries

This map shows how every countries perform economically from 1960 to 2017. United States, China, Japan, Australia and Malaysia have an annual growth GDP of 2.03%, 6.84%, 3.14%, 1.89% and 3.88% respectively.

GDP of Selected Countries

Relationship between GDP per Capita and Government Spending

Government spending does affect the GDP per Capita but only for country with less population like United States. For densely populated country like China, government spending is not a big factor on GDP per Capita. From the chart, when the government spending is greater, the GDP per Capita tend to be greater too.
